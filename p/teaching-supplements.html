Group discussion: New technologies in transport planning

1. What are the most critical challenges that the transportation sector will face in the immediate future?

2. What future technologies are needed to address these challenges?

One of the greatest challenges is the number of players. many who are affecting those decisions…and then you are dealing with planning horizons that are short and long term (easily into 20 years) and policy planning in 50 years. How do you best guide this kind of decision making that has so many players involved from local to federal? How do you engage in new ways for adaptation? You can’t put the brakes on…Investments continue to be made and projects continue to move forward. How do you inform that decision making? This is one of the key challenges.

2. How can we actually convince people to trust the system?

Group discussion: Transport modelling

1. Assume that you can develop the most advanced transport model of all time, what new infrastructure would you model/simulate to improve the traffic efficiency or safety in Auckland? This can be a new bridge, highway, tunnel or extension of a current infrastructure.

2. What kind of data would you collect to show the improvements in the traffic condition?

Group discussion: Transport modelling

Around 200,000 visitors come to Auckland every year for the Lantern festival, bringing great cultural exchange and revenue, but also creating major traffic problems through traffic congestions, traffic incidents, crowdedness in public transport systems and road closures.

Suppose that you can have the access to a large telecommunication data that shows the locations and trajectories of tens of thousands of people in Auckland during the festival, what would you do with the data to help solving the traffic issues in Auckland during the event? (assume that you have all the technical skills/resources required to do what you want to do but consider the ethical and personal confidentiality issues in your answer)
