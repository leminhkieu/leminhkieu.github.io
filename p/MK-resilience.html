Why Transport Resilience matters in New Zealand

🌏 Seismic Risk
One of the most earthquake-prone nations on Earth — Kaikōura (2016), Christchurch (2011)
🌀 Climatic Hazards
Cyclone Gabrielle (2023): 225,000+ properties without power, major transport paralysis
🏙️ Urban Concentration
~35% of NZ's population in Auckland
🚗 Rapid Technological Shift
Autonomous and shared mobility are entering the network now before resilience frameworks exist

The window for embedding resilience into the next generation of transport is narrow — and it is open right now.

From reactive to proactive resilience

❌ Reactive (Traditional)

  • Respond after disruption strikes
  • Manual, slow infrastructure repair
  • Evacuation plans based on static assumptions
  • Mobility data collected after the event

✅ Proactive (AI-Enabled)

  • Model behaviour before disasters occur
  • Simulate recovery to guide investment
  • Dynamically re-route AVs during crises
  • Optimise evacuation in real time
AI and simulation are not merely tools for optimising everyday operations
, but are foundational to building transport systems that anticipate, absorb, and recover from shocks.

Classical approaches in modelling human mobility during disasters

  • Treat mobility events as independent
  • Miss cascading effects between locations
  • Can't capture recovery dynamics
  • Ignore category-specific responses

Point Process

Probabilistic generative models for modelling sequences of events with complex temporal dependencies

$\lambda(t) = g(t)$

Classical Hawkes process

$\lambda(t) = \mu + \sum_{t_i} \alpha \cdot e^{-\beta(t-t_i)} $

Excitation: the intensity increases by $\alpha$ whenever an event occurs and then exponentially decays towards the baseline level $\mu$

Our proposed Disaster-Aware Hawkes process

$\lambda(t, c) = \lambda_0(t, c, r) \cdot (1 + B(t, c)) + \sum_{(t_i, c_i) \in \mathcal{H}_t} \alpha(c_i \rightarrow c) \cdot w(t_i, d_i) \cdot \kappa(t - t_i)$

+ Specific category: $c$

+ Excitation strength from category $c_i$ to category $c$: $\alpha(c_i \rightarrow c)$

+ Bounce-back effects: $B(t,c)$

+ Weight function based on dwell time $d_i$ at visit $i$: $w(t_i, d_i)$

+ Decay kernel that models how excitation diminishes over time: $\kappa(t - t_i)$

Parameter estimation using Maximum Likelihood Estimation ( L-BFGS-B)
