Crash Analysis System (CAS) data

The Crash Analysis System (CAS) maps traffic crashes reported to us by the NZ Police and is built on the ArcGIS system. You can also interact with the data as a data table. The main page of the Crash Analysis System (CAS) features a map and information.

User guide: https://opendata-nzta.opendata.arcgis.com/documents/NZTA::crash-analysis-system-cas-data-user-guide/about

Crash Analysis System (CAS) data

What can you do for the Assignment?

Data cleaning and processing

Show basic statistics on the data

Show the impacts of different variables on traffic crashes, e.g. weather, lighting condition, type of traffic crashes, vehicles involved, number of lanes, crash severity, traffic control

Show us at least 3 most interesting patterns from your dataset

Crash Analysis System (CAS) data

What can you do on for the Final Project?

Refinement of the 3 most interesting patterns from the Assignment

Geo-spatial analysis: Where are the hot-spots of crashes in your region? What can we do to make the situation better?

Time-series analysis: How do crashes change over time in your area? Is this getting better, or is there anything we can do?

Clustering analysis: Group similar crashes. What are those clusters? what can we do for each cluster of crashes?

Residential Property Sales Statistics data

Data files for New Zealand house sales from CoreLogic (previously Property IQ New Zealand). Elements include geographic location (excluding house number), sale date and price, capital and land value, building floor area, land area, building type.

Data dictionary and examples: https://www-library-auckland-ac-nz.ezproxy.auckland.ac.nz/eproducts/NZ_housedata/CSTDAT8700_DataDelivery_20210607.xlsx

Residential Property Sales Statistics data

What can you do for the Assignment?

Data cleaning and processing

Show basic statistics on the data

Show the impacts of different variables on the house prices in your region, e.g. land area, building condition, type of cladding, number of bedrooms

Combine with other types of data, e.g. NZ derivation data, School decile rating, to explain the house price in your region

Show us at least 3 most interesting patterns from your dataset

Residential Property Sales Statistics data

What can you do on for the Final Project?

Refinement of the 3 most interesting patterns from the Assignment

Geo-spatial analysis: Where are the areas of high and low house price in your region of interest?

Time-series analysis: How do price change over time in your area? You may discuss gentrification of areas, link to building quality, building floor area, landscaping quality, etc.

Clustering analysis: Group similar properties. What are those clusters?
