Project 1: Identify sites for temporary bike lane

Levels of walking and cycling have increased significantly during the COVID-19 pandemic

Pop-up for temporary bike lanes have been a recent initiative

E.g:
https://www.uci.org/news/2020/pop-up-bike-lanes-a-rapidly-growing-transport-solution-prompted-by-coronavirus-pandemic

However, it is challenging to know how we can reshape cities to facilitate safer cycling

Project 1: Identify sites for temporary bike lane

This project will identify potential roads in Auckland where a part of the roadway can be reserved for a bike lane

This will faciliate safer cyclying and encourage more people to cycle

Learn & use GIS tools, data analytics and visualisation

Project 2: Modelling evacuation on public transport terminals

A Part IV project in 2020 has developed a simulation model of the Brittomart Station to evaluate evacuation time in various scenarios

This project will further develop this model by adding real data from potentially the AT Hop Card data and more scenarios of evacuation

Project 3: A digital twin of Auckland: Analyse the impacts of hypothetical changes in road infrastructure

A/B Street is a model/game of city simulation developed for the Seattle road network

Link:
https://github.com/dabreegster/abstreet

Project 3: A digital twin of Auckland: Analyse the impacts of hypothetical changes in road infrastructure

This project will adapt this exciting tool for the road network in Auckland and investigate the impacts of changes in road infrastructure to the cars, bikes and pedestrians in Auckland
