Traffic studies of the future: Video processing

Discussed by Don McKenzie of Stantec as the futute of data collection in transport

Project 1: Video data collection and analysis

We have developed a video processing AI for you to play with, example here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aWRvpY8Mh1I

Project plan: (1) Video record traffic in Auckland in two locations: one motorway from a pedestrian bridge, and one street from a building

(2) Use our developed software to process the recorded videos into traffic data: Speed, Flow and Density

(3) Compare the processed data with data from TomTom for validation

Project 2: Analysis of potential traffic safety threats from shared paths

You will work with a PhD student who is also working at the Auckland Transport, Dr Doug Wilson and me on this project

Project plan: (1) Video record two sites of shared paths between cyclists, pedestrians and micro-mobility users in Auckland

(2) Use our developed software to process your recorded video into trajectories of shared path users

(3) Identify near-missed and safety threats from the processed trajectories and the video

(4) Answer transport policy questions: Is shared path really dangerous? Do we need to separate them? Do we need to send cyclists and micro-mobility users to the main roads?

Project 3: Develop an interactive data dashboard for traffic data using Python

Auckland has a traffic dashboard to show real-time traffic data, but looking quite dull: https://www.journeys.nzta.govt.nz/auckland/traffic-dashboard

Could we make something more visually pleasing like the Covid-19 dashboard?: https://www.aucklandnz.com/auckland-index/covid-19-dashboard
