My learning theories

Constructivism is a theory in education that recognizes learners construct new understandings and knowledge, integrating with what they already know.

Based on the idea that people actively construct or make their own knowledge

The origin of the theory is also linked to Jean Piaget's theory of cognitive development.

Piaget talked about four stages in human development; the sensorimotor stage, the preoperational stage, the concrete operational stage and the formal operational stage.

Just like us, students bring their own unique experiences to class

Their background and previous knowledge impact their learning curve

The two key concepts within the theory are accommodation and assimilation.

Assimilating means incorporating new experiences into old experiences, rethinking and altering perceptions.

On the other hand, accommodation reframes new experiences and it keeps changing as the context changes.

Teachers should be able to use "constructivist learning theory" to help their students approaching the knowledge

First: knowledge is built upon other knowledge, learning is a contextual

Second: learning is an active process, people learn to learn as they learn.

Third: learning is a social activity.

Forth: motivation is the key to learning.
